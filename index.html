Hello, I'm Tawfik Osman

I am a PhD student at Arizona State University, supervised by Professor Ahmed Alkhateeb. I completed my MSE in Electrical Engineering at Arizona State University in May 2021. I am currently a graduate research assistant at Wireless Intelligence Lab.

Download CV

Research

My research focuses on building frameworks for digital twin-assisted mmWave beam management, vision-aided wireless communication models, and other innovations in cutting-edge wireless technologies. I am part of the Wireless Intelligence Lab, where we work on developing state-of-the-art solutions for challenges in wireless communication and AI applications.

Research Interests:

  • Machine Learning in Wireless Communication
  • Vision-Aided Models for Beam Training
  • Wireless Communication Channel Estimation and Modelling

Research Activities

  • Built a framework for digital twin-assisted mmWave beam management on real-time testbeds and standard-compliant research platforms.
  • Implemented a 5G standalone (SA) setup using OAI open-source code, software-defined radios from Ettus Research, and mmWave RF front-ends for FR1 and FR2 bands.
  • Developed vision-aided mmWave beam tracking algorithms for vehicle-to-vehicle (V2V) communication scenarios using DeepSense6G datasets.
  • Contributed to the design of a multi-modal testbed for acquiring wireless and sensor datasets in DeepSense6G, enabling the development of AI models for 6G research.
  • Leveraged deep learning techniques to map sub-GHz channel state information (CSI) to mmWave beam vectors using real dual-band testbed datasets.
  • Built an end-to-end communication testbed for field evaluations of prototype sub-6GHz and mmWave Reconfigurable Intelligent Surfaces (RIS).

Prior to this, I completed my MSE in Electrical Engineering at Arizona State University in May 2021, where I focused on predicting mmWave beams using sensory data, such as GPS and RGB images. My project highlights include:

  • Integrated GPS-RTK receivers and stereo camera sensors into a sub-6GHz testbed for real-world vehicle-to-vehicle communication studies.
  • Developed a machine learning model leveraging pre-trained ResNets and fully connected neural networks, utilizing datasets that combine vision, GPS, and wireless signals.

For more details on my work and contributions, visit my LinkedIn or Google Scholar profile.
